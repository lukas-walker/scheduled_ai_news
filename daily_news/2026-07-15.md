# AI News — 2026-07-15

- **Ada Lovelace Institute proposes a "global AI governance floor"** — Ahead of the UN Global Dialogue on AI Governance opening in Geneva this month, the Institute published a submission arguing for minimum global standards grounded in existing human rights law rather than full harmonization. The three-layer framework covers substantive minimum content, shared risk-evidence infrastructure, and process commitments like state reporting and civil-society participation. [Source](https://www.adalovelaceinstitute.org/blog/the-case-for-a-global-ai-governance-floor/)
- **Epoch AI: frontier models show no sign of learning from repeated play** — Epoch's new EBR-bench has AI systems replay the complex board game Earthborne Rangers up to 30 times to test whether they improve with experience; so far none show meaningful gains despite being given rules, a card database, and the ability to take notes. The result adds concrete evidence to open questions about whether current models can learn on the fly, with implications for both economic and safety forecasts. [Source](https://epoch.ai/publications/earthborne-rangers-benchmark)
- **Mystery AI Hype Theater dissects "AI bubble" bailout arguments** — Episode 80 hosts Open Markets Institute fellow Matt Scherer to unpack his report "No Bailout for Big Tech Billionaires," which pushes back on arguments that governments should rescue AI companies if the investment bubble bursts. Hosts Bender and Hanna frame it as a preview of the policy fights likely to follow any AI market correction. [Source](https://dair-institute.org/maiht3k/)
- **Andrew Ng: pair CS fundamentals with agentic-coding skills** — In The Batch, Ng lays out three feedback loops for building AI-native products — agentic coding (minutes), developer steering (hours), and market feedback (days) — arguing that developers who want to stay competitive need to master agent-driven workflows on top of solid fundamentals. [Source](https://www.deeplearning.ai/the-batch)
- **Fireship rounds up a crowded frontier-model release week** — A new video covers the pileup of GPT-5.6, Grok 4.5, Claude Fable 5, Sonnet 5, an Nvidia supply "crisis," and new humanoid robots, giving a fast overview of how the model landscape reshuffled in early July. [Source](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lVqFpykEa8Y)
