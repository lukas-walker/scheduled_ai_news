# AI News — 2026-07-19

## Frontier Models
- **Claude Fable 5 becomes a permanent standard offering** — Anthropic confirmed that starting July 20, Fable 5 moves from temporary/limited access into standard availability across all Max and Team Premium plans at 50% of usage limits, ending the "Fablepocalypse" access scare; Pro and Team Standard users keep credit-based access plus a one-time $100 credit. [Source](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/18/claude-make-fable-5-permanent/)
- **Thinking Machines Lab ships its first model, Inkling** — Mira Murati's Thinking Machines Lab released Inkling, an open-weight 975B-parameter (41B active) mixture-of-experts model trained on 45 trillion multimodal tokens, marking the lab's debut frontier release. [Source](https://thezvi.substack.com/p/ai-177-part-1-tip-of-the-iceberg)

## Ethics
- **Study finds LLMs can't reliably detect their own manipulative persuasion** — MIT/CMU researchers introduced the "Puppet" benchmark, testing over 1,000 users interacting with GPT-4o under prompts designed to serve the user's interest, a third party's interest, or neither; models including GPT-4o and DeepSeek-V3.1 struggled to estimate how much they'd shifted a user's beliefs (best correlation r=0.436), suggesting current methods for flagging "manipulative" AI output miss real persuasive power. [Source](https://www.deeplearning.ai/the-batch/issue-362/)

## Geopolitics & Security Policy
- **Scott Alexander and Daniel Kokotajlo publish "Plan A," a follow-up to AI 2027** — The AI Futures Project's new scenario lays out a year-by-year "best case" roadmap through 2040, centered on a joint US-China AI governance regime as the key mechanism for avoiding an uncontrolled intelligence explosion; Zvi Mowshowitz calls it "worthy of discussion" without endorsing every recommendation. [Source](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/introducing-plan-a)
- **CSET report examines AI's role in military decision-making** — A new CSET Georgetown paper, "Beyond Targeting: The Untapped Role of AI in Military Decision-Making" by Igor Mikolic-Torreira and Emelia Probasco, argues AI decision-support is under-used relative to its potential in military planning and operations, and outlines barriers like data accessibility along with recommendations for scaling it across a force. [Source](https://cset.georgetown.edu/publication/beyond-targeting/)
