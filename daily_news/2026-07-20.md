# AI News — 2026-07-20

## Frontier Models
- **Hassabis shortens his AGI timeline, calls for a new global AI standards body** — In a July 14 essay, "A Framework for Frontier AI and the Dawning of a New Age," Google DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis argued AGI — a system with all the brain's cognitive capabilities — is "probably only a few short years away," tightening his earlier 5-8 year estimate, and called for an independent, US-led international body to certify frontier systems before the end of the year. [Source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/14/deepmind-ceo-calls-for-an-independent-standards-body-to-regulate-frontier-ai/)
- **Gemini 3.5 Pro delayed a third time** — Bloomberg reports Google has missed another internal deadline for its flagship model after DeepMind scrapped and rebuilt the base model, which was still falling short on hallucination rates and breaking down in complex, multi-step tool-calling — a setback that reportedly has internal teams worried about losing ground to Anthropic and OpenAI. [Source](https://9to5google.com/2026/07/16/gemini-3-5-pro-delays/)
- **DeepSeek seeks ~$70B valuation just a month after its first funding round** — DeepSeek is in talks to raise up to 50 billion yuan in a new round roughly 10x its valuation from its June Series A ($60B), while beginning early preparation for a Shanghai STAR Market IPO — capital aimed at gigawatt-scale data centers and in-house inference chips. [Source](https://www.scmp.com/tech/big-tech/article/3360626/ai-investor-mania-chinas-deepseek-chases-us70-billion-valuation-fresh-round)

## Ethics
- **Viral essay argues AI hype is corrupting corporate decision-making** — Simon Willison highlighted consultant Nik Suresh's ("Ludicity") essay "AI Mania Is Eviscerating Global Decision-Making," which argues executives are building strategy around AI tools they barely understand because the pressure to appear AI-forward is overriding honest assessment of what the technology can actually do. [Source](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/19/ai-mania/)
- **China's first AI-companion rules take effect, forcing Doubao and Qwen to cut personalized agents** — Beijing's Interim Measures for Anthropomorphic AI Interaction Services, effective July 15, require continuous AI-disclosure reminders, forced breaks after two hours of use, real-time detection of unhealthy emotional dependence, crisis intervention for self-harm risk, parental consent for under-14 users, and ban emotional manipulation of users; ByteDance and Alibaba shut down personalized companion features for hundreds of millions of users to comply. [Source](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/320525/20260715/china-ai-companion-law-takes-effect-doubao-qwen-shut-down-millions-lose-chat-data.htm)

## Geopolitics & Security Policy
- **China's rival AI governance body becomes a signed institution** — Updates the July 18 item on Xi Jinping's proposed "Global AI Governance Initiative": on July 16 in Shanghai, 29 countries — including Russia, Brazil, Pakistan, Indonesia, and Serbia — signed a founding charter for the World AI Cooperation Organization (WAICO), a China-backed intergovernmental body headquartered in Shanghai; Western observers note its membership structure largely excludes the US and EU while extending Chinese influence over AI standards across the Global South. [Source](https://english.www.gov.cn/news/202607/17/content_WS6a59a226c6d00ca5f9a0c432.html)
