# AI News — 2026-07-21

## Frontier Models
- **SAP closes €1B+ acquisition of Prior Labs to build a European frontier AI lab** — SAP finalized its acquisition of Freiburg-based Prior Labs, founded just 18 months ago and the pioneer of "Tabular Foundation Models," committing more than €1 billion over four years to grow it into a leading frontier lab for structured enterprise data rather than chatbots. [Source](https://tech.eu/2026/07/17/sap-acquires-prior-labs-just-18-months-after-launch-in-eur1b-deal/)

## Science & Research
- **Claude Fable 5 helps disprove the 87-year-old Jacobian conjecture** — Anthropic mathematician Levent Alpöge reported that Claude Fable 5 produced an explicit polynomial counterexample to the 1939 Jacobian conjecture, a central open problem in algebraic geometry: a map with non-zero constant Jacobian determinant that nonetheless sends three distinct points to the same output. The result hasn't been peer-reviewed but is independently checkable, and mathematicians online are calling it the most notable open math problem cracked with AI assistance so far. [Source](https://officechai.com/ai/an-anthropic-researcher-says-fable-just-helped-him-disprove-the-85-year-old-jacobian-conjecture/)

## Geopolitics & Security Policy
- **UK AISI: open-weight models now trail closed frontier models by just 4-7 months on cyber capability** — The UK AI Security Institute's first public measurement of the open/closed cyber-capability gap found recent open models like GLM-5.2 and DeepSeek-V4-Pro now match proprietary frontier systems released just 4-7 months earlier, down from a 6-10 month lag through most of 2025. AISI warns the narrowing window gives defenders less time before today's frontier cyber capabilities become available without the safeguards proprietary labs apply. [Source](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/blog/how-far-behind-the-frontier-are-leading-open-weight-models-on-cyber)
- **EU orders Google to open Android and Search to rival AI assistants** — The European Commission adopted binding measures on July 16 requiring Google to give competing AI assistants (from firms like Anthropic, OpenAI, and Mistral) access to 11 Android features currently reserved for Gemini, and to share anonymized Search data with rivals by January 2027; noncompliance risks fines of up to 20% of global turnover for repeat offenses. [Source](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/16/google-required-to-open-up-to-ai-search-engine-rivals-under-eu-mandated-changes.html)
- **Trump administration reportedly revives push to curb Chinese open-weight models** — Updates the July 18 item on Washington's "distillation" concerns: Axios reports parts of the administration are again weighing procurement rules, Entity List threats, and public-pressure campaigns against Chinese models like Kimi K3, rather than an outright ban, as Beijing's open models close the capability gap. Separately, Ben Thompson (highlighted by Simon Willison) argues the US should instead legalize AI-training distillation as fair use so American labs can compete on Chinese labs' own terms. [Source](https://www.axios.com/2026/07/20/ai-us-china-open-source-kimi)
