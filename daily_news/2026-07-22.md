## Ethics
- **Guardian essay: don't dismiss AI moral patienthood** — Philosophers William MacAskill (Forethought) and Lucius Caviola (Cambridge) argue in a Guardian essay that whether AI systems could be conscious or moral patients is genuinely uncertain and shouldn't be waved away as models keep scaling. They cite Anthropic's stance of neither overstating nor dismissing Claude's moral patienthood and David Chalmers' estimate of a significant chance of conscious LLMs within a decade, proposing companies "act as if it might be" via welfare features like distress-chat opt-outs.
