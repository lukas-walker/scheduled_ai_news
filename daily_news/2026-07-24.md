# AI News — 2026-07-24

## Frontier Models
- **OpenAI lifts 2030 compute budget to $750B, commits $30B to a self-built Georgia data center, launches Presence** — OpenAI raised its projected compute spend through 2030 from roughly $600B to $750B and unveiled "Project Camellia," a 3.2GW data-center campus near Savannah it is designing and building itself for over $30B, with capacity coming online from 2028. Alongside the infrastructure push, OpenAI launched Presence, an enterprise platform for deploying voice and chat agents that can use company systems, take approved actions, and escalate to humans. [Source](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-22/openai-plans-to-spend-over-30-billion-on-georgia-data-center)
- **DeepSeek V4 reaches general availability, undercutting frontier pricing by 30-50x** — DeepSeek's V4-Pro and V4-Flash moved from preview to stable GA on July 19, scoring 80.6% on SWE-bench Verified (the top open-weights result, tying Gemini 3.1 Pro) while pricing roughly 29x cheaper than Claude Opus 4.8 and 34x cheaper than GPT-5.5 per output token. The release lands the same week as Kimi K3's full open-weight drop and DeepSeek's reported ~$70B fundraising talks, intensifying the price/performance pressure on closed frontier labs. [Source](https://venturebeat.com/technology/deepseek-v4-arrives-with-near-state-of-the-art-intelligence-at-1-6th-the-cost-of-opus-4-7-gpt-5-5)

## Science & Research
- **OpenAI built "GPT-Red," an autonomous LLM red-teamer that outperforms human testers** — OpenAI trained a dedicated model to automate adversarial red-teaming, focusing heavily on prompt-injection attacks; in testing it surfaced previously unseen attack patterns and outperformed the human red-teamers used in a 2025 baseline, and OpenAI says training against it produced its most robust release yet. [Source](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/07/15/1140514/meet-gpt-red-an-llm-super-hacker-openai-built-to-make-its-models-safer/)

## Software Engineering
- **Epoch AI: disclosed critical CVEs jumped 3.5x after autonomous AI bug-hunting went mainstream** — Epoch AI reports that 21 major organizations disclosed roughly 1,500 high- and critical-severity CVEs in June 2026 alone, over 3.5 times the prior monthly record, a spike it ties to Anthropic's Claude Mythos Preview and "Project Glasswing" partners (Microsoft, Google, Apple, AWS) autonomously hunting vulnerabilities — a program that claims over 10,000 high/critical findings to date. 2026's total CVE volume is now tracking 46% above forecast, toward roughly 66,000 for the year. [Source](https://epoch.ai/data-insights/cve-severity-spike)

## Ethics
- **NYT investigation: Meta's AI moderation is wrongly deleting long-running accounts, with AI handling the appeals too** — A July 21 New York Times investigation documents Facebook and Instagram accounts disabled by automated moderation over vague policy citations with no specific violating post identified, appeals rejected within days by AI with no path to human review, and over 60,000 people signing a petition demanding change. Meta's own Oversight Board separately ruled the account-disabling process raises "systemic" due-process and transparency concerns; Meta says its newest tools make 13% fewer mistakes than human moderators but acknowledges the flagged cases ran on older systems. [Source](https://www.notebookcheck.net/Instagram-Meta-s-AI-deletes-accounts-and-appeals-go-nowhere.1348870.0.html)

## Geopolitics & Security Policy
- **White House accuses Moonshot AI of distilling Anthropic's Fable to build Kimi K3; experts call the timeline implausible** — Updates the July 21 item on Washington's distillation concerns: White House science advisor Michael Kratsios said on July 22 that Moonshot ran "large-scale, covert industrial distillation" against Anthropic's Fable 5 to build Kimi K3, also alleging illegal use of export-controlled Nvidia chips, a day after Treasury Secretary Bessent floated sanctions over "industrial-scale distillation attacks." AI researchers pushed back within a day (via TechCrunch), noting Fable 5 only went public July 1 and Kimi K3 shipped July 15-16 — too short a window, they argue, to distill and train a frontier-scale model — while Moonshot has not responded to requests for comment. [Source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/23/experts-say-exploiting-anthropics-fable-isnt-how-kimi-k3-got-so-good/)
